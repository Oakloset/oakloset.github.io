Scope and definitions

This policy applies to all OaKloset users, regardless of the country where they live, complying with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR, EU 2016/679) wherever applicable.

"Personal data" means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person.

1. Who we are

OaKloset ("we") is a mobile wardrobe-management app that uses artificial intelligence to organise clothing and suggest outfits. This policy explains what personal data we process, for what purpose, and what your rights are.

2. What data we collect

Category Data Source
Account Email, password (stored encrypted — bcrypt hash, never in plain text), preferred language Provided by you
Wardrobe content Photos of items, AI-generated attributes (category, colour, style, occasion, tags), wardrobes/personas Provided by you + automated processing
Activity Logged outfits, wear dates, requested suggestions, streaks and badges Generated by your use
Approximate location Coordinates used to fetch the weather forecast — only if you enable weather-aware suggestions Device (with your permission)
Notifications Push notification token, preferences (enable, reminder time, time zone) Device
Technical / diagnostics Error reports and anonymous/pseudonymised usage metrics Automatic

We do not collect special categories of data (health, ethnicity, etc.) and we do not ask for your address, date of birth, or sensitive payment data (payments, where they exist, are processed by the Apple/Google stores and/or RevenueCat, not by us).

3. Why we use the data and on what legal basis

  • Provide the service (manage your account, store your wardrobe, log outfits) — performance of a contract (Art. 6(1)(b)).
  • Generate outfit suggestions and categorise photos with AI — performance of a contract; image processing is necessary to the core feature.
  • Weather forecast for suggestions — consent (you enable location); revocable at any time.
  • Push notifications (daily reminder) — consent; can be disabled in settings.
  • Improve the app and fix errors (diagnostics, metrics) — legitimate interest (Art. 6(1)(f)), with pseudonymisation.
  • Communications about the beta/news — consent (separate opt-in).

4. Artificial intelligence

Item photos and wardrobe data are sent to the AI model provider (Anthropic, Claude) to extract attributes and generate suggestions. This data is processed solely to fulfil the request and is not used to train models under the API's commercial terms. See sub-processors in section 8.

  • The photos are meant for clothing items. If an image includes people (for example, a worn outfit), that data is also processed by the AI. Do not upload photos of third parties without their consent, or images you do not want processed this way.
  • AI-generated results (categories, tags, outfit suggestions) may contain errors or inaccuracies and should be treated as mere suggestions — the decision is always yours.

5. Where we store the photos

The photos you upload are stored on Amazon S3, on infrastructure in the European Union (eu-west-1, Ireland), with encryption at rest and transmission protected by HTTPS. Access is restricted to what is strictly necessary to operate the service. When you delete an item or your account, the corresponding photos are removed from storage.

6. Local storage and identifiers

The app stores on your device the information needed for it to work:

  • Session tokens, in the system's secure storage (Keychain on iOS / Keystore on Android).
  • A local copy (cache) of your data, so the app responds smoothly and works with intermittent connectivity.
  • Push notification token, if you enable reminders.

For usage metrics we use a pseudonymised identifier linked to your account (see section 8). We do not use advertising cookies and do not share identifiers for advertising or commercial profiling purposes.

7. How long we keep it

  • Account data and content: for as long as the account exists.
  • After account deletion: we delete your data (including photos in storage) — see section 9. Backups are purged within 30 days.
  • Diagnostics/metrics: retained for a maximum of 90 days.
  • Non-selected beta applicants: deleted at the end of recruitment.

8. Who we share with (sub-processors)

We do not sell personal data. We use providers acting as sub-processors, under data processing agreements (DPAs) and, wherever possible, hosted in the EU:

Sub-processor Purpose Location

We only record actions (for example: create account, sign in, add item, log outfit, generate suggestion, view the subscription screen) and associated metadata; we never send your photos or wardrobe content.

Transfers outside the EEA are protected by the European Commission's Standard Contractual Clauses.

9. Your rights (GDPR)

You have the right to: access, rectification, erasure, restriction, objection and portability of your data, and to withdraw consent at any time.

The app lets you exercise two of these directly, on the profile screen:

  • Export my data — download a file with all your data (portability, Art. 20).
  • Delete account — permanently deletes the account, photos and associated data (Art. 17).

For the remaining rights, or complaints, contact wintercodeunip@gmail.com. We will respond to your requests within a maximum of one month, which may be extended by two further months for complex cases (with prior notice), under the GDPR. You may also lodge a complaint with the competent supervisory authority (in Portugal, the CNPD — cnpd.pt).

10. Security

Encrypted passwords (bcrypt); short-lived session tokens with rotation and revocation; HTTPS communication; restricted, private database access; rate limiting; security headers (helmet). No system is 100% secure, but we apply technical and organisational measures appropriate to the risk.

11. Children

OaKloset is not intended for children under 16 without parental authorisation, where required by applicable law. We do not knowingly collect data from children; if we become aware of it, we delete it.

12. Changes

We may update this policy. Material changes will be communicated in the app or by email. The date at the top indicates the version in force.
